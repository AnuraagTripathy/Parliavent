# Parliavent mock data

## Motion

Should cities ban cars from downtown areas?

## Seed argument

Cities need to ban cars downtown. Every time I go there's traffic, honking, and someone almost gets hit. If we don't ban cars now, downtowns will become unlivable death zones. Europe does it and their cities are way nicer.

## Findings

### Finding 1: Clarity

Type: clarity

Span:
unlivable death zones

Title:
This reads as venting

Reason:
Naming a concrete risk lands harder than a phrase readers may discount.

Suggested rewrite:
downtowns will keep getting more dangerous for people on foot

Actions:
- Use suggestion
- Keep as-is

### Finding 2: Claim needing evidence

Type: claim

Span:
Europe does it and their cities are way nicer

Title:
This needs a source

Reason:
A reader can wave this off without evidence behind it.

Actions:
- Attach source
- Mark as opinion
- Keep as-is

Suggested sources:
1. Car-free streets and city livability — Urban Planning Review — sample source
2. Oslo city center traffic reduction outcomes — City of Oslo policy brief — sample source
3. Paris low-traffic zones, three-year review — sample case study

Important:
Label these as sample sources in the UI.

### Finding 3: Fallacy

Type: fallacy

Span:
If we don't ban cars now, downtowns will become unlivable death zones

Plain-language title:
This offers only two extremes

Fallacy name:
False dilemma

Confidence:
82%

Reason:
Ban now or disaster are not the only options. Congestion pricing, better transit, and partial bans sit in between.

Simple example:
"Either we ban all phones in school or students will never learn."

Suggested rewrite:
Without action on downtown traffic, conditions for pedestrians may keep getting worse.

Actions:
- See fix
- Use this wording
- Keep as-is
- Dispute
